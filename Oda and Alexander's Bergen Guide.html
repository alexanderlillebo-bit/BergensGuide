IV · Chapter Four

Transport

❋

How to get to the apartment from the airport — and how to move around Bergen once you're here.

From the Airport

Flesland Airport (BGO) is about 20 km south of the city.

Public Transport

Take Bybanen (the Bergen city rail) to Byparken (about 50 minutes). From Festplassen N — right across from Byparken — change to bus line 10 towards Mulen and get off at Øvre Sandviksvei, then a short walk to the apartment.

Cost: 51 NOK · Buy tickets in the Skyss app

Taxi or Uber

A direct ride from the airport to the apartment, with fixed taxi prices.

Travel time: 20–30 minutes (depending on traffic)
Daytime (06–18): approx. 490 NOK + fees
Evenings, nights & weekends: approx. 540 NOK + fees

Getting Around Bergen

Bergen is compact and walkable — but the bus is your best friend for longer distances.

Public Transport

Download the Skyss app — buy tickets and plan journeys in one place. Line 10 stops about 3 minutes from the apartment and runs frequently to the city centre.

Single ticket: 51 NOK

We generally do not recommend taxi or Uber from the apartment to the city centre — the bus runs often and is just as fast.

Other Ways to Get Around

Walking: Bergen is small and very walkable.
Cycling & electric scooters: Available for rent around the city.

Parking

Street parking is available outside the apartment. Use the EasyPark app to pay.

Paid hours: Mon–Sat 08:00–23:00 · Sundays and holidays free

V · Chapter Five

Things to Do

❋

Cultural landmarks, museums, and historic places — the essential Bergen experiences. Tap each card to read more.

01 Bryggen UNESCO World Heritage +

The historic Hanseatic wharf of Bergen, dating back to the 14th century. Walk through narrow wooden alleyways between the iconic colourful buildings, browse small artisan shops, and feel the layers of history. The whole area is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Best time: Early morning or late afternoon to avoid the cruise ship crowds.

02 Fisketorget — The Fish Market Food market +

Bergen's open-air and indoor fish market, where you can taste fresh seafood, smoked salmon, and local specialities. Touristy but lively, and a good place to grab lunch with a view of the harbour.

Note: Prices can be high — best for tasting, not large meals.

03 Mount Ulriken Cable car · Highest peak +

The highest of Bergen's seven mountains. From the top you have miles of views over the sea, fjords, and mountains. Take the Ulriken Cable Car up for an easy visit — or hike up the 1,333 Sherpa steps if you want a challenge. See the Hikes chapter for more.

Access: Ulriken Express Bus from the Fish Market (April 1 – September 30, about 15 minutes), or take Bybanen (the light rail) line 2 from Byparken to Haukeland.

04 Mount Fløyen Funicular · Views +

The most accessible of Bergen's mountains, with the famous Fløibanen funicular taking you to the top in minutes. Once there: walking trails, a playground, a troll forest for kids, and excellent views over the city. Café at the top.

Tip: You can also walk down (or up) in about 45–60 minutes.

05 Gamle Bergen Open-air museum +

An open-air museum of about 50 wooden houses from the 18th and 19th centuries, preserved as a small "old Bergen" village. Easy walking distance from the apartment in Sandviken, and a wonderful glimpse of life in old Bergen.

Best time: May to September.

06 KODE Art Museums Art · Four buildings +

One of the largest art museums in the Nordic countries, spread across four buildings around Lille Lungegårdsvann lake. Strong collection of Edvard Munch, J.C. Dahl, and contemporary art. Easy to spend half a day here.

Tip: One ticket gives access to all four KODE buildings.

07 Bergenhus Fortress Medieval · Free entry +

One of Norway's oldest and best-preserved stone fortresses, dating from the 1240s. Walk around the grounds for free, or visit Håkon's Hall and the Rosenkrantz Tower for a deeper look into medieval Bergen.

Location: Right next to Bryggen — easy to combine.

08 The Hanseatic Museum & Schøtstuene History · Bryggen +

A small but fascinating museum about the Hanseatic League and the German merchants who ran Bryggen for centuries. Schøtstuene, the assembly rooms, are nearby and included with your ticket.

Note: Check opening hours — sometimes closed for renovations.

09 Edvard Grieg Museum — Troldhaugen Music · Composer's home +

The home of Norway's most famous composer, Edvard Grieg, just outside the city. Visit his villa, the small composer's hut by the lake, and the modern concert hall where lunchtime concerts are held in summer.

Access: Light rail (Bybanen) to Hop, then a 20-minute walk or short taxi.

10 St. Mary's Church Bergen's oldest building +

The oldest building still in use in Bergen, built around 1150. A Romanesque-Gothic stone church with a beautifully carved Baroque pulpit. Right next to Bryggen — small but worth the visit.

Tip: Combine with Bryggen and the fortress for an easy half-day.

VI · Chapter Six

Walks

❋

Slow strolls through the neighbourhoods we love most.

Walk 01

Sandviken

Charming coastal village

A historic fishing village with many 18th century wooden houses — very charming and very 'Bergen'.

Route highlights

  • Start from the apartment
  • Wander through old cobblestone streets and white wooden houses
  • Stop for coffee or a drink at Falske Reker
  • Pick up fresh pastries at Bakeri 29
  • Walk along the waterfront and enjoy the fjord views

Best for — Photography, peaceful exploration, local atmosphere

Walk 02

Nordnes

Historic peninsula with local character

A picturesque area that juts out into the fjord. Today it is a sought-after residential neighbourhood with families, schools, and leafy parks — feeling more like a small town than an inner-city suburb.

Route highlights

  • Explore narrow alleyways only locals know about
  • Stop at Løvetann Kafé & Bistro for coffee and organic, vegetarian food
  • Visit Nordnesparken for fjord views and the totem pole (a gift from Seattle)

Best for — History, local vibes, quiet neighbourhoods away from Bryggen

Walk 03

University Area & Botanical Gardens

Peaceful heights

Ascend to Bergen's academic quarter and the botanical garden — a calm, green walk on the city's hill.

Route highlights

  • Walk up to the Botanical Garden (Muséhagen), located just outside the university area
  • Explore the different garden areas — rare trees, mountain plants, and seasonal flowers
  • Pick up coffee at Godt Brød
  • Stroll through Nygårdsparken, the largest park in Bergen — with the iconic duck pond and winding paths under mature trees

Best for — Nature lovers, peaceful walks, families with children

Walk 04

Fjellveien

Flat ridge walk with city views

One of the most famous walking routes in Bergen — and the flattest. You get a lovely view of the Bergen mountains, forests, historic buildings, fjord, and cityscape.

What to know

  • The 4.6 km path offers panoramic views over Bergen
  • Mostly a pretty path through the woods, with regular city views
  • Popular with all ages — joggers, couples, and families with prams
  • You can walk the full path, or do a section and return to the centre

Best for — All ages and fitness levels, views without steep climbs, peaceful forest walks

VII · Chapter Seven

Hikes

❋

Bergen sits between seven mountains — go and meet them.

Hike 01

Mount Fløyen

Easy & scenic

One of the seven mountains surrounding Bergen. The funicular takes you to a beautiful area with many walking and hiking options.

Route options

  • Easy: Take the Fløibanen funicular from the city centre right to the top and enjoy the views. There is a charming café (Fløistuen) for fresh pastries.
  • Moderate: Walk up to Fløyen (45 minutes to 1 hour depending on your pace).
  • Playground: For kids, there is a large playground and the mysterious troll forest.

Best for — Families, easy walks, city views

Hike 02

Stoltzekleiven

Short & steep

A steep paved trail and stairway with approximately 908 steps up to Sandviksfjellet — one of the most popular trails in Bergen.

What to know

  • About 315 metres of elevation gain
  • Not an easy hike if you're not used to climbing
  • As you gain altitude, the views get better
  • From the top — beautiful views over Sandviken and Byfjorden
  • You can continue from the top all the way to Mount Fløyen

Best for — Fitness enthusiasts, a quick challenge, Sandviken views

Hike 03

Mount Ulriken

Highest peak

The highest of Bergen's seven mountains. From the top you have miles of views over the sea, fjords, and mountains.

Route options

  • Easy: Take the Ulriken Cable Car to the top. There is a restaurant, café, and shop.
  • Active: Hike up the Sherpa steps (1,333 steps). Allow 60–90 minutes.

Access: Ulriken Express Bus from the Fish Market (April 1 – September 30, about 15 minutes), or take Bybanen (the light rail) line 2 from Byparken to Haukeland.

Best for — All fitness levels (via cable car), scenic views, shorter hikes

Hike 04

Vidden

The mountain plateau

One of the most popular hikes in Bergen, with beautiful views across the city and region. Between Mount Ulriken and Mount Fløyen you can see the fjords and islands around Bergen. On a clear day you can even see the Folgefonna glacier.

What you need to know

  • About 18 km from Ulriken to Fløyen — takes 5–6 hours
  • Start and end with the Ulriken Cable Car and Fløibanen funicular
  • Well-marked trail with stunning high-mountain terrain

Best for — Full-day hikers, experienced hikers, those wanting Bergen's best views
